Hi, I'm Gizem — I study memory and decision-making.

Ph.D. Candidate, Psychological & Brain Sciences @ Washington University in St. Louis. My research examines value‑directed remembering, recognition memory, and how retrieval incentives shape sensitivity, confidence, and bias—often with pupillometry and drift‑diffusion modeling.

Research

Dissertation

Retrieval incentives and recognition memory

Across experiments, I test whether gain vs. loss framing at retrieval selectively enhances memory sensitivity, alters response caution, and shifts bias. Designs integrate trial‑level feedback, confidence, and surprise tests, paired with drift‑diffusion analysis.

Pupillometry

Task‑evoked pupil responses during value shifts

I model how trial‑by‑trial value changes modulate early constriction vs later dilation during encoding, separating automatic arousal from strategic effort using growth‑curve modeling and mixed‑effects analyses.

Metacognition

Confidence, heuristics, and value source memory

When do value tags get retrieved versus inferred? I examine confidence‑driven response tendencies and their consequences for adaptive control.

Methods

Signal detection & diffusion models

I combine SDT and DDM to decompose accuracy and RT: drift rate (evidence quality), boundary separation (caution), and starting point (bias).
